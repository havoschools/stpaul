Tuition & Financial Aid

St. Paul promises an unparalleled educational experience for your child that will be foundational for their academic, social, and spiritual life in the years to come. We are committed to creating an inclusive environment for all students, including various financial and socioeconomic backgrounds. With approximately 15% of our current student body receiving some form of need-based financial aid, St. Paul is fortunate to have an effective financial assistance program, making the St. Paul education available to many.

Tuition for the 2024-2025 school year is as follows:
3-day junior kindergarten $10,900
5-day junior kindergarten $15,430
kindergarten - 6th grade $22,175

SAGE Dining Lunch fees:
3-day junior kindergarten $125
5-day junior kindergarten $180
kindergarten-6th grade $1,160

School supplies and consumables are estimated at $500 per year.

 

If you have any questions about need-based aid, please contact Kay Strom, Director of Financial Aid.

Scholarship Opportunities

  • The Shepherd’s Fund
  • Ken Cheeseman Scholarship

The Shepherd’s Fund

Launched in 2015, the Shepherd’s Fund is designated for families with financial need who have at least one parent working in full-time Christian ministry. The Shepherd’s Fund is intended to supplement the amount between St. Paul Christian Academy’s financial aid award and a family’s ability to pay.

Ken Cheeseman Scholarship

The Ken Cheeseman Scholarship is awarded to financially qualifying diverse students. It is intended to supplement the amount between St. Paul Christian Academy’s financial aid award and a family’s ability to pay. God creates all of His children in His image (Genesis 1:26-27) and endows them with unique talents and differences. Embracing these differences helps create a dynamic school environment that reflects the broad Kingdom of God. The mission of St. Paul Christian Academy is best accomplished through a racially, ethnically, and socio-economically diverse community.

If a student qualifies for The Shepherd's Fund or the Ken Cheeseman Scholarship, it will supplement the amount between the financial aid awarded and the amount a family pays toward tuition.

For more information, contact Kay Strom at kstrom@stpaulchristian.org.
